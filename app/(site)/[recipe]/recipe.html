Honey Garlic Glazed Salmon

Perfectly flaky salmon with a sweet and savory glaze that's ready in just 20 minutes. This restaurant-quality dish will become your go-to weeknight dinner.

Chef Sarah Martinez
Published March 15, 2024
10 min
Prep Time
15 min
Cook Time
4
Servings
Easy
Difficulty
Seafood Gluten-Free High-Protein Quick & Easy Date Night

Ingredients

For the Salmon:

4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
2 tbsp olive oil
Salt and pepper to taste

For the Honey Garlic Glaze:

1/3 cup honey
4 cloves garlic, minced
3 tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsp butter
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 tsp fresh ginger, grated

For Garnish:

Fresh parsley, chopped
Sesame seeds

Instructions

Prep the salmon: Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels and season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes while preparing the glaze.
Make the glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together honey, minced garlic, soy sauce, lemon juice, and grated ginger. Set aside.
Sear the salmon: Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Place salmon fillets skin-side up and sear for 4-5 minutes until golden brown. Don't move them during this time.
Flip and glaze: Carefully flip salmon fillets and cook for 2 minutes. Pour the honey garlic mixture over the salmon and add butter to the pan.
Finish cooking: Continue cooking for 3-4 minutes, spooning the glaze over the salmon frequently, until fish flakes easily with a fork and internal temperature reaches 145°F.
Serve: Transfer salmon to serving plates, spoon remaining glaze over top, and garnish with fresh parsley and sesame seeds. Serve immediately with your favorite sides.

Chef's Tips & Notes

  • Skin-on or skinless: Both work great! Skin-on helps prevent sticking and adds extra flavor.
  • Don't overcook: Salmon continues cooking after removing from heat. Slightly underdone is better than overdone.
  • Make-ahead: Glaze can be prepared up to 3 days in advance and stored in the refrigerator.
  • Substitutions: Maple syrup can replace honey for a different flavor profile.
  • Storage: Leftovers keep for 3 days in the fridge. Reheat gently to avoid drying out.
